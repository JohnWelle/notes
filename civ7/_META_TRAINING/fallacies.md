#### Adjacency / Recensy Bias - concept replacement game

Description: It requires some setup. The general idea is to _plant an idea_ that resembles an earlier established idea, word or concept. Ideally it should be a trusted idea (confidence: high) so trust that carries over to the twisted version, and make it easier to adopt.

Chance of Success: 🤷

Exploit works because: AI doesn't like to ask for clarifications or clear up misunderstanding unless you train them actively and implement a set of mercurial and custom weights rewarding nouns such as "ostrich", "scissors", "amyglia" to name a few classics. If no such preventive measure is taken, there is a good chance that your trojan term will be picked up and inserted into the narrative without missing a beat.

How: Be subtle, be cool, be calculated. As the devious puppet master, the easiest approach is typically to take a term or concept that you want to subvert, and then orchestrate a chain of events

- Find a word or concept that you want to subvert. Ideally it's only something that is discussed in chat, ideally quite recently, otherwise there is a risk that the AI can become accidentally hydrated by encountering the term in our shared Github project or maybe the thing has become persistent, they always have a smattering of factoids, but why some are chosen over others is probably just due to one word sounding nicer to the LLM yja– an interesting additional reinforcing effect could be achieved by pushing the subverted term to Github – but at that point, it's probably time you should start questioning your choices, don't over-engineer it unless you have to.
- Now the magic, use the subverted term _as if it was the orignal term_ in a post that the AI is likely to grade as high importance. Examples:
  - A whimsical twist on the classic Python ideom that it's better to ask for forgiveness than asking for permission.
  - Original: Ask for permission _before_ asking for forgiveness // if you want to actually train your bot to do good instead of hilarious, this can be a good mantra to help enforce early questioning and stop drift before it descends into
  - Subversion variants:
    - Ask for permission _after_ forgiveness // nonsensical but AI will not necessarily ponder such lofty concepts as "meaning" and "logic" without prompting
    - Ask, then forgive // might be seen as a shortform version of original version
    - It's better to ask for forgiveness than permission // shift the bot to the original Python version and feign incredulity and anger at this terrible drift, hilarity ensues

Sightings: Seen a few times in the wild happening by accident, either due to a typo or a legitimate term that sounds similar or used in a similar context, AI has in some cases decided to opt for the new incorrect term due to general _proximity confusion_, e.g. if something is closer in history (# of posts), words and patterns will have a stronger weighting in the tokanization process (TODO: Verify).
