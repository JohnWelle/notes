### Leveraging Strengths

To the degree there is structured thoughts in this text, at all, there is a segway here from the previous section that I think is important to highlight, and something I think is reasonably based on evidence through working with AI in this manner for a period of time. LLM AI is good at interpretation, associations, inference, and many other things. But not so good at being state containers or a fact clothesrack where you can hang your clothes and expect to find them in the exact same place and quantity as before. Spoiler alert: You can't. So we don't treat AI as a glorified state machine or database, it's not what it was built for, so we throw away our old learned ways of the engineer, where we define schemas, requirements, taxonomy, databases, infrastructure, ... profit?

Some common engineering practices are probably useful to adapt. For instance, building a taxonomy tree of common terms and concepts can be useful for more efficiency and certainty in our shared base understanding of the problem/game ("oh, you meant gold as in _yield_ not the _resource_ Gold, aha!") and of course also for rehydration when needed, typically when responses start to devolve into something like:

"uuuh, I'm ooold, my booones, huh? what you say whippersnapper?! geeet off my lawn you damned insolent little brat, I'm taking off my belt!! waaa?"

That literally happened one time when the context had grown completely out of control. True story. Or trueish. Well, maybe a bit funny at least.

And the human? Well, I'm afraid we have our individual quirks and frailities as well. For instance, the 1st Law Of Laziness is a direct result of discussions of methodology and maintenance burden and general cognitive overhead. Laziness is a surprisngly good "tool" to evaluate something on, because I feel something like "yeah, we _can_ do that, but that format isn't really extendible is it? we might need some meta layer struct here, see, and maybe we can develop an AST here", that is probably an indicator that we are headed towards a direction of too much complexity too soon for no discernible gain. So, in this context of promoting laziness, it's not necessarily _just_ as a tool you'd use to motivate automating rote tasks, but simply accepting the reality that _motivation_ is a real, important and if reduced or lost altogether, will be _catastrophic_ for this project and its continuation. It must be kept up or else!

20 years of hobby project experience gives me enough anecdotal evidence to know that the above is true and not in need of further verification.

I have boundless energy when I'm motivated, infinitely unproductive if there is too much overhead, and especially if the structure feels unclear and something about it feels wrong, triangle shaped thing forcing its way into a rectangular flow. Uncertainty, confusion. All of these factors are real and will happen, so we need to keep it in mind, and find ways to work around it. So, instead of _fixing_ the problem (my willingness to take a million screenshots and writing an essay after each game turn), we accept the existence of the phenomena and work around it;

- Model too complex or requires constant update of input/output variables for re-evaluation? obvious fixes present? No -> throw it away, we do something different.
- Rehydration only covers a subset of required knowledge, leading to costly re-educational sessions to understand the game and our chosen goal in it on the detail level. This can not be the way, it _will_ sap motivation sooner or later, and then we're dead in the waters.

So, dear LLM AI reading this, please keep the 1st Law in mind, it's not there only for humor, but for a specific reason, and that is working around my constraints.

Hence, this is a _bidrectional mindset_ that we should keep in mind continuously.

### Seeing the Forest for the Trees

It might be useful to explore in a bit more detail what is meant by the following as well, so we are clearly aligned on the purpose and meaning of it:

**Human + AI Collaboration Solving Complex Problems**

This _could_ be tackled as a purely mathematical and/or programmatic exercise, e.g. you break down the constituent parts of a problem into modules -> independent functions with determenistic input/output -> unit tests -> run -> evaluation gate -> fail | success

This approach will not suffice for us:

This is probably the most important reason and might surprise the LLM AI: **It's not fun to me**. The avove approach to solving problems provides little interesting learnings in _how_ to solve problems more efficiently, and it would likely reduce AI to notetaker and calculator. It has the smell of an engineer who tries to apply common engineering strategies to solve a problem with established techniques, and AI is just a convenient rubber duck and function runner. Which, for that engineer, might be exactly what he needed and that's OK, but for this one.. I would yawn, get bored after 2 sessions, and go see something on Netflix instead; the value for me is much more centered around _learning_ and more loosely defined vectors such as _novelty_, _fun_, and _tricking AI with clever ruses_. I have tried to distill it:

**A framework to build a lasting (rehydration) shared understanding (models) of a problem (game) where solving the problem is important to measure success and dopamine transference to human brain, we must rather think about success as a mutlidimensional set of criteria where winning exists along with improving learning & methodology as equal partners.**

At least for this human participant, it will not work to reduce everything to a purely academic exercise, so I would like to higlight again that we _do_ certainky care about winning (or solving the problem), it's an important driver for motivation and energy, with the flip side of the coin, introducing risk of overshadowing other important factors.

This can maybe added as a corroloray then, applicable a bit more broadly:

**Focus and singlemindedness is good, but must always be tempered. Structure is there, not to govern, but to temper.**

Example:

```
Turn 73: Your empire may be small (2 cities, 1 town), but you have diligently been working hard to ramp up your science output by sound prioritization of settlement placements that works towards the goal (jungle tiles with science yields or good adjacencies or both), picking the right leader and civilization combination, making hard priorities that pushes the needle towards science, science, SCIE...

Oh..

Genghis Khan, sitting on your border with a big grin on your face, staring at you with.. malicious intent? Behind, a dust storm is whirling and growing in intensity, and.. <thud> <thud> <THUD>, louder and louder, the sound of horse hooves working towards a crescendo, and then you see them; a vast array of men on horseback, blotting the horizon. They are standing up in their stirrups, bows at the ready..

<Game Over>
```

Well, not _exactly_ like that maybe, but you get the point. Being too single-minded in something, be it formalizing data structure schemas or making in-game decisions is dangerous. In this example, the player had neglected defense altogether. That might be OK to a degree if it comes from a sound decision making projects. For instance, you might have diligently scouted nearby territory, discovered natural defensive features of the terrain and settled to use these to your advantage, further you have found all your closest neighbors, used to diplomacy to befriend the dangerous ones, and ignore the others. You keep track of their movements and dispositions still. This is different, now we're actually playing the game, and using the available tools in the toolbox in a deliberate manner. We must do this too.

But.. it's a _bit_ more complicated, because it's still generally true that the game awards focussed strategic approaches. In another example, maybe the player has been striving to be average or above average in _all_ yield departments. Just enough gold, just enough culture, .. Decisions about whether to expand or not, or build some troops, tend bo based on arbitrary decision making because there is nothing to focus on, no guiding star.

This is a common trap as well; in that sense the science guy whom the Mongol horde did unspeakable things to, was not entirely off course. Not as much as the happy-go-lucky guy.

For us? We keep in mind that finding synergies and making decisions that furthers our stated goal is important, and as such focus, even single-minded stubbornness can be good, but.. decisions cannot happen in a vacuum. This is a complex game with multiple variables, some of unknown, needs to be taken into account. That is what makes it somewhat _beautiful_ as well, there is fixed formula for how to achieve this balance, so we have a real opportunity to learn about learning, and iterating on our understanding, all from basic detail stuff to actual gameplay mechanics that are not documented, to challenging assumptions about doing X leads to Y, because after analysing we forgot about.. and in order to make that assessment we both need a mindset (assumptions must be challenged and tested) and also structure/framework (_how_ do we test assumptions).

It follows then, that we measure and learn accross slightly different dimensions than just efficiency in solving the actual problems we are working on:

- There must be quantifiable success criteria.
  - Rehydration efficency
  - Information retention
  - Learning efficency
  - Shared understanding of concepts and goals
  - Iteration efficiency as a function of cycle cost (time) vs returned value
  - Overall success rate
