## Age Transition

- For this phase, there was an initial plan in place along, namely switch to Hawaii as civilization since those guys are mighty crazy beneficial when it comes to coastal empires. HUGE. Anyway, more on that elsewhere.
- Win condition: Survey our empire and decide on the most likely win condition to succeed. In this case, it's perhaps surprisingly BOTH culture and science on the table, and with military on the backburner and economy as 'meh, possible but boring'. Why each can work:
  - Culture: This is the obvious one, we lead in culture, Hawaii or most likely pick is very strong in culture, we have twice as much as number two, easy pick eh? Maybe, but.. we are not winning culture by culture alone, to win in next age we need to be 2-4x better _in tourism points_ than the second best contender. Whether it's 2-4 depends on when in the age you are: Half the age: 4x, and then in intervals it drops to 3x, 2x. We are not that far ahead at all in tourism, so selecting culture is just not coasting on the lead it's to specifically consider what we can do to increase our tourism conversion to the max, enough to be able to make 2X at least, not the same thing. I think we _can_ but it's important to be aware of.
  - Science! But what about that funky brainac Ashoka??! He mad good at sciences!? Yes, but.. 1) he will almost surely drop quite low in the beginning of next age, looks like its entirely reliant on urban infrastructure, scientists and bonuses from attributes. All of those will dry empty initially, however, it can (and probably) will recover. But there _will_ be a window for us to get our infra up more quickly and go balls wall on science if we want to take over lead and possibly keep the lead if we are single-minded enough. 2) That's a big if, but.. there is also the fact that the science win condition _must_ be completed by fulfilling a few key milestones. The AI traditionally is super bad at _not_ floundering and be sharp in aiming for and completing these goals. So we can afford lower science to a degree if we beeline more sharply to what we need to win. So is on table is.
  - Military: We currently lead in this category and is not at all impossible to win, but that would certainly mean looking into other civs that are militarist experts, such as Bulgaria or Mongolia, and make a shift in mindset to think war first, everything else secondary. City State befriendment is now a tool to be weighed against using Diplomacy features such as espionage, denouncements, strategic friendships and much more. CS's can have strategic benefits too, so targeting military CS to get combat related bonuses become much more prioritized. Culture/Science is nice insofar it gives us mightier men. Etc. Etc. But it's definitely possible.
- But.. ended up with boring, reliable Culture, though actually regretting it a bit now, could have been a more fun challenge with the others, but oh well!
