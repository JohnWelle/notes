# Tecumseh + Tonga → Hawaii Session Charter

## Purpose

This session tests whether a Tecumseh + Tonga opening can exploit a rural-first coastal economy built around:

- Fish / marine tiles
- Volcano-adjacent rural improvements
- City-state suzerainty
- Wide settlement count
- Strong Age transition economics

The main objective is not merely to win, but to learn whether this strategy is robust after patch 1.4.1.

## Game Setup

- Game: Civilization VII
- Version: 1.4.1
- DLCs: All
- Platform: GeForce NOW
- Map: Fractal Continents
- Size: Large
- AI Players: 9
- Independent Powers: 24
- Leader: Tecumseh
- Antiquity Civilization: Tonga
- Exploration Civilization: Hawaii
- Disaster intensity: Catastrophic

## Strategic Premise

Tecumseh gains unusually high value from Independent Powers / City-States.

Tonga provides ocean-traversing scouts in Antiquity, allowing contact with many more Independent Powers than normal.

The run therefore combines:

1. Early ocean scouting.
2. Aggressive city-state acquisition.
3. Coastal/fishing economy.
4. Volcano-enhanced rural tiles.
5. Wide expansion.

## Core Experiment

Can a rural-first fish + volcano economy transition into Exploration better than a more conventional Antiquity urban-specialist engine?

Important: do not compress this into "rural survives better" without explanation.

The relevant mechanics are:

- Rural improvements can remain useful through the Age transition.
- Urban buildings remain but may lose adjacency value.
- Specialist yields in 1.4.0+ depend heavily on adjacency values.
- Deprecated buildings may continue creating maintenance drag.
- Volcano eruption yield bonuses reset at Age transition.
- Fish/fishing boats are rural, but have separate bonuses and losses.

## Working Hypotheses

### H1 — Rural-first reduces transition drag

Status: plausible.

Reason:

- Less dependence on Antiquity adjacency-specialist engines.
- Fewer deprecated buildings carrying maintenance while producing reduced value.
- More value held in tile improvements rather than urban stacks.

Do not treat this as universally true. Strong adjacency buildings can still be worth building.

### H2 — Fish meta is real

Status: promising.

Reason:

- Fishing boats and marine resources can produce high-value yield mixes.
- Production + science fish tiles are especially valuable.
- Hawaii strongly amplifies coastal/marine play.

### H3 — Volcanoes are strong but reset-sensitive

Status: confirmed/pending measurement.

Reason:

- Eruptions can add yields to nearby tiles during an Age.
- These accumulated eruption bonuses reset at Age transition.
- Volcanoes should be evaluated separately from rural persistence.

### H4 — Ashoka is the primary strategic rival

Status: active watch.

Reason:

- Ashoka ended Antiquity with very strong science.
- His style may align unusually well with current rural/happiness meta.
- AI may still misplay execution.
