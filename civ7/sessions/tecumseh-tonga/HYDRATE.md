## Mechanics Needing Careful Handling

### Age Transition: Urban vs Rural

Do not summarize as "rural survives better" without detail.

The main issue is not simply rural vs urban.  
The key issue is dependence on Antiquity adjacency-specialist engines.

Urban-heavy specialist engines can be hit hard because:

- Buildings remain.
- Maintenance remains.
- Antiquity adjacency bonuses reset.
- Specialist yields in 1.4.0+ depend heavily on tile/building adjacency values.
- Result: the empire may keep much of the cost while losing much of the output.

This run avoided heavy Antiquity urban building stacks, reducing obsolete maintenance drag.

### Rural Improvements

Rural improvements are important, but do not overstate.

- Rural improvements can continue functioning through transition.
- Rural-heavy play reduces reliance on collapsing specialist engines.
- This is not universally optimal.
- Strong rural/urban buildings can still be worth building if their payoff before transition is high.

### Fish

Fishing boats are rural improvements, but should be analyzed separately.

Important known effects:

- Antiquity-specific bonuses can disappear, e.g. Altar +1 Production on Fishing Boats.
- Tonga/Hawaii/marine bonuses may alter value significantly.
- Fish tiles with Production + Science are especially high value.
- Compare yield composition, not just total yield.

### Volcanoes

Volcanoes are separate from rural persistence.

- Eruptions can add yields during an Age.
- Accumulated eruption yields reset at Age transition.
- Do not call this stable "volcanic fertility" in Civ terms.
- Volcano tiles may still be worth prioritizing because they can rebuild value during the new Age.

### Specialists

Specialist decisions must use marginal yield, not total tile yield.

Correct method:

- Use the tooltip's + / - specialist delta.
- Compare that delta against the best rural tile the population could otherwise work.
- Do not compare the final urban tile total against a rural tile total.
